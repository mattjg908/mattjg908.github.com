About Me

Resume

I'm a graduate student (MS in Computer Science) with 10 years of software engineering experience. I enjoy all BEAM languages, currently my day job is Elixir, my passion is Erlang, and my hobby (not BEAM related) is the Plan 9 flavor of C.

I have contributions into Elixir-Lang, Nerves (embedded Elixir), 9front (Plan 9 fork), snmp-elixir (Hex package), and many others. I authored Harvard University's CorpusBuilder Ruby Client Gem and I have a few of my own libraries!

I have worked for a number of high profile clients such as Netflix, Google and Harvard. I am a former Wall Street options trader of 5 years, English teacher, and mechanic. I invented a Nerves-powered Raspberry Pi device which solves a major issue in data centers- so far over 5,000 units of the prototype have sold.

I need to step away from the computer from time to time... outside of coding I do motorcycle stunts (dirtbike, msx), spin wrenches on vintage compact pickups, and read Native American history.

Operating Systems

Embedded Software (Nerves, GRiSP)

At Cato Digital (Virtual Power Systems) I gained experience with embedded systems, specifically Nerves and GRiSP. I also became an expert in industrial communication through my exposure there to SNMP, Modbus, IPMI, ZMQ (not an industrial protocol) and a variety of Erlang libraries and tools to work with these protocols.

Erlang

Currently, I work at the world-famous Erlang Solutions with industry greats, celebrity programmers, kind-hearted, bright-minded, BEAM-loving innovators. Lots of interesting projects, I'm learning a lot about Kubernetes, AWS, Docker, and how the BEAM and modern virtualization and containerization are a great match.
