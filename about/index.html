About Me

Resume

I am a remote developer with 10 years of experience in software, specializing in Elixir and Erlang. I'm also a stuent in Drexel University's MS of Computer Science program.

I'm an open-source contributor and author of Harvard University's CorpusBuilder Ruby Client Gem, I've also contributed to Elixir-Lang, and Hex packages like snmp-elixir and Nerves. I even have a few of my own libraries out there ! I have worked for a number of high profile clients such as Netflix, Google and Harvard. Formerly a Wall Street options trader (silver and gold), an English teacher, and a motorcycle mechanic. Lately I've been reading Testing Elixir and practing speed reading techniques.

I invented a Raspberry Pi device, powered by Nerves, which solves an industry-wide issue in data centers. Essentially, the most widely used communication protocols are insecure and outdated (Modbus TCP, IPMI, most SNMP versions, etc.). The device I invented allows intelligent control of power in data centers while maintaining security as a priority and still supporting the use of the common but insecure protocols. So far over 5,000 units of the prototype have sold.

Of course I also need to step away from the computer from time to time... outside of coding I do motorcycle stunts on my dirtbike and my msx. I also repair and restore vintage Japanese minitrucks like the Nissan D21 Hardbody and Gen 1 Toyota Tacomas.
