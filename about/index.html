About Me

Resume

I am a remote developer with 10 years of experience in software, specializing in Elixir and Erlang. I'm also pursuing a graduate degree in Drexel University's MS of Computer Science program. I'm an open-source contributor and author of Harvard University's CorpusBuilder Ruby Client Gem. I've also contributed to Elixir-Lang, and Hex packages like snmp-elixir and Nerves. I enjoy doing test driven development, learning new tricks with PostgreSQL, and I love pair programming. I have worked for a number of high profile clients such as Netflix, Google and Harvard. I have been a Wall Street options trader (silver and gold), an English teacher, and a motorcycle mechanic. Lately I've been reading Testing Elixir and practing speed reading techniques.

Of course I also need to step away from the computer from time to time... outside of coding I do motorcycle stunts on my dirtbike and my msx. I also repair and restore vintage Japanese minitrucks like the Nissan D21 Hardbody and Gen 1 Toyota Tacomas.

Elixir, Erlang

When I interviewed for DockYard, Chris McCord (author of the Phoenix framework) was one of the people who interviewed me. Over the couple years that I worked there, collaborating with the great Elxir team, I gained exposure to Elixir/Phoenix.

This experience was improved upon later working at Virtual Power Systems where I wrote Erlang and Elixir and developed a prototype of a Nerves device which subsequently sold 5,000 units. The device is in use in data centers in the Western US, and that project is still under active development and may change the face of data centers as we know it.

Currently, I work at Erlang Solutions with industry greats such as Francesco Cesarini, Robert Virding, and a number of other very talented Elixir and Erlang developers.
